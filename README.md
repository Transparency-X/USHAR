# USHAR

This is a comprehensive project plan for the **Unified Sonic Harm & Atrocity Registry (USHAR)**. This plan integrates the individual/personal scope of "Sonic Harm" with the political/human rights scope of "Sonic Atrocity" into a single, interoperable framework.

Given the complexity of acoustic violence—which ranges from neighbor harassment to state-sponsored torture and genocide—this plan adopts a **Multi-Tiered Forensic & Legal Framework**. It treats sound not merely as noise, but as a kinetic weapon and a vector for psychological and physiological injury.

---

# Project Plan: Unified Sonic Harm & Atrocity Registry (USHAR)

## 1. Executive Summary
**Objective:** To establish a centralized, legally admissible registry and index that documents, classifies, and facilitates the prosecution of sound-based crimes across the spectrum of personal harassment to crimes against humanity.
**Core Philosophy:** Sound is a continuous spectrum of force. The mechanism of injury in domestic sonic harassment is technically and physiologically related to sonic torture; only the *intent*, *scale*, and *perpetrator status* differ. USHAR bridges this gap to enable cross-domain pattern recognition and resource mobilization.

## 2. Taxonomy & Classification Framework
Before data collection, a unified ontology must be established to map overlaps between personal harm and political atrocity.

### 2.1 The Sonic Violence Matrix
| Dimension | Sonic Harm (Individual/Personal) | Sonic Atrocity (Political/Systemic) | Overlap/Commonality |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Intent** | Harassment, stalking, revenge, coercion | Torture, genocide, intimidation, crowd control | Psychological destruction, sleep deprivation, forced displacement |
| **Equipment** | Consumer audio, DIY infrasound, directional speakers | LRADs, industrial transducers, sonic cannons, broadcast infrastructure | Directionality, frequency manipulation, volume/intensity |
| **Scale** | Single victim, household, neighborhood | Detention centers, protest sites, entire regions | Cumulative trauma, physiological damage (hearing loss, tinnitus, PTSD) |
| **Perpetrator** | Individuals, HOAs, rogue contractors | State actors, military, police, paramilitaries, terrorists | Impunity, gaslighting, denial of harm |
| **Legal Domain** | Nuisance, assault, stalking, civil torts | CAT, Geneva Conventions, ICC Statutes, ECHR | Human rights violations, bodily integrity breaches |

### 2.2 Crime Typology Index
The registry will categorize incidents into four primary clusters:
1.  **Acoustic Harassment & Stalking:** Targeted noise campaigns against individuals/families.
2.  **Sonic Torture & Ill-Treatment:** Use of sound in detention, interrogation, or institutional settings.
3.  **Weaponized Crowd Control & Suppression:** Military/police use of acoustic devices causing permanent injury.
4.  **Sonic Terrorism & Genocidal Acts:** Broadcasted hate speech combined with traumatic noise, or environmental sonic destruction aimed at ethnic/cultural erasure.

## 3. Operational Architecture

### 3.1 Data Intake & Verification Pipeline
*   **Multi-Modal Evidence Collection:** Accepts audio recordings, decibel/frequency logs, medical audiograms, psychological evaluations, witness testimony, and equipment schematics.
*   **Forensic Audio Lab:** A dedicated unit to authenticate recordings, separate signal from noise, identify specific equipment signatures (e.g., distinguishing an LRAD from a modified subwoofer), and assess physiological impact potential.
*   **Victim-Centric Trauma-Informed Intake:** Specialized case workers trained in both domestic abuse and torture survivor care to prevent re-traumatization during reporting.

### 3.2 The Dual-Registry Database
*   **Public Index:** Anonymized, aggregated data for researchers, journalists, and policymakers. Maps hotspots, equipment trends, and legal outcomes.
*   **Secure Forensic Vault:** Encrypted, chain-of-custody compliant storage for raw evidence intended for law enforcement, courts, and international tribunals.
*   **Equipment Registry:** A technical database of known sonic weapons and harassment devices, including manufacturer specs, import/export records, and modification guides used by perpetrators.

## 4. Engagement Strategy: From Local to Global
This section addresses how victims/targets engage resources to cease harm and prosecute offenders.

### 4.1 Local & National Level (Law Enforcement & Courts)
*   **Standardized Police Protocols:** Develop "Sonic Assault Response Guides" for local police, treating targeted noise as potential assault/battery rather than mere nuisance. Includes mandatory decibel logging and equipment seizure protocols.
*   **Judicial Expert Witness Network:** Maintain a roster of acousticians, audiologists, and human rights experts who can testify to the *weaponization* of sound in domestic and criminal courts.
*   **Civil Litigation Support:** Provide template filings for restraining orders and civil suits that specifically cite sonic harm metrics and medical causation.
*   **Regulatory Advocacy:** Lobby for updates to municipal noise ordinances to include *frequency-specific* limits and *intent-based* enhancements (e.g., "malicious acoustic projection").

### 4.2 Regional Level (Human Rights Bodies & Interpol)
*   **Regional Human Rights Commissions:** Submit shadow reports linking domestic sonic harassment patterns to state-tolerated atrocities. File petitions for systemic failures to protect against sonic torture.
*   **Cross-Border Equipment Tracking:** Collaborate with regional customs and Interpol to track the illicit trade of dual-use acoustic devices used in both domestic abuse and state repression.
*   **Asylum & Refugee Support:** Provide documented evidence of sonic persecution to support asylum claims where applicants flee state-sponsored sonic torture or genocidal sonic campaigns.

### 4.3 Global Level (UN, ICC, International NGOs)
*   **International Criminal Court (ICC):** Compile dossiers meeting the threshold for Crimes Against Humanity or War Crimes when sonic atrocities are part of widespread/systematic attacks.
*   **UN Special Rapporteurs:** Feed verified data to Rapporteurs on Torture, Freedom of Expression, and Indigenous Rights. Advocate for a UN resolution recognizing "Malicious Acoustic Projection" as a distinct human rights violation.
*   **Global Sanctions & Export Controls:** Advocate for listing specific sonic weapon manufacturers and distributors under Magnitsky-style sanctions when their products are linked to atrocities.
*   **International Standards Body:** Push for ISO/IEC standards defining "safe exposure limits" for non-consensual acoustic exposure in civilian contexts.

## 5. Victim/Target Mechanism Pathways
How individuals navigate the system based on their situation:

1.  **Immediate Cessation:** Emergency hotline → Dispatch of mobile acoustic monitoring unit → Real-time injunction/restraining order filing → Equipment confiscation.
2.  **Medical & Psychological Recovery:** Referral to specialized clinics understanding sonic trauma → Documentation of injuries for legal proceedings → Long-term therapy funding access.
3.  **Criminal Prosecution:** Evidence package handoff to prosecutor → Expert witness assignment → Victim advocacy during trial.
4.  **Systemic/Political Redress:** Aggregation of individual cases into pattern evidence → Submission to national human rights institutions or international bodies → Public advocacy campaign.

## 7. Risk Management & Ethics
*   **Re-Traumatization:** All intake processes must be trauma-informed. Victims retain ownership of their data and can withdraw at any time.
*   **Misuse Potential:** The equipment registry must be curated to avoid serving as a "how-to" guide for perpetrators. Technical details should be restricted to verified researchers/law enforcement.
*   **Jurisdictional Conflicts:** Establish clear protocols for cases spanning multiple legal domains (e.g., a state actor harassing a dissident abroad). Prioritize victim safety over jurisdictional purity.
*   **Data Security:** End-to-end encryption, air-gapped forensic vaults, and strict access controls to protect victims from retaliatory surveillance.
*   **Scientific Rigor:** All claims of harm must be backed by peer-reviewed science or validated forensic methodology to maintain credibility in court and policy arenas. Avoid amplifying unverified conspiracy theories while remaining open to emerging acoustic threats.

## 8. Success Metrics
*   **Documentation:** Number of verified cases indexed across all four typologies.
*   **Cessation:** Percentage of reported active harms successfully stopped via intervention.
*   **Accountability:** Number of prosecutions initiated, convictions secured, or sanctions imposed using USHAR evidence.
*   **Policy Change:** Legislative/regulatory reforms enacted at any level citing USHAR data.
*   **Victim Outcomes:** Self-reported improvement in safety, health, and access to justice among registry participants.

---

### Critical Note on Overlap Recognition
This plan explicitly rejects siloing "personal" and "political" sonic violence. A key operational principle is **Pattern Escalation Monitoring**: When multiple "personal" sonic harassment cases in a region share equipment signatures or tactics with known state atrocity programs, USHAR flags this as potential *state-tolerated or state-proxied violence*, triggering automatic escalation to regional/global engagement pathways. This ensures the registry captures the full continuum of sonic harm as requested.
